Claude Sonnet 5 Becomes the Default Model for All Claude Users

Anthropic launched Claude Sonnet 5 on June 30, 2026 and switched it to the default model for every Free and Pro Claude user starting July 1 — replacing the previous default. Sonnet 5 performs close to the flagship Claude Opus 4.8 on many tasks but at introductory pricing, making it the most capable widely-available model at scale. Cowork, Claude Code, and the Claude Platform all transitioned simultaneously.

Why it matters: Fastest-ever mass upgrade to model capability; productivity tools built on Claude get an automatic performance jump with no cost change for users.

Google Gemini 2.5 Pro Deep Think Surpasses Rivals on Science Benchmarks

Google released Gemini 2.5 Pro with a new Deep Think reasoning mode that achieves 82.4% on GPQA Diamond and 89.8% on MMLU-Pro, surpassing OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and Anthropic's Fable 5 on published science benchmarks. The model uses parallel thinking techniques and a two-million token context window. GPT-5.6 Sol/Terra/Luna preview details were also confirmed in parallel, with Sol achieving new highs on Terminal-Bench 2.1.

Why it matters: The frontier AI race is accelerating faster than market participants expect; companies with multi-model strategies (cloud providers, enterprise software) face rapid differentiation pressure.

Anthropic Begins Samsung Talks on Custom 2nm AI Accelerator

Anthropic has entered preliminary discussions with Samsung Electronics to manufacture a custom AI accelerator chip, leveraging Samsung's 2nm process and advanced packaging. The Information reported the exploratory talks on July 2. Separately, global startup fundraising hit a record $510 billion in H1 2026, with AI companies including OpenAI and Anthropic dominating. Anthropic also agreed to a California state-wide deployment deal: Claude at 50% discount to every California state agency and participating cities, the largest US state government AI deployment in history.

Why it matters: Custom silicon reduces Anthropic's dependence on NVIDIA; Samsung benefits from a major fabless customer; California deal signals enterprise/government AI spend accelerating sharply.

Fable 5 Restored After US Government Lifts Export Control Order

Anthropic's most powerful model, Fable 5, went offline on June 12 after a US Commerce Department export control order. The controls were lifted June 30 and Fable 5 restored to global access July 1 across Claude.ai, Claude Platform, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork — a 20-day outage. Google's 2025 environmental report separately disclosed a 37% year-over-year increase in electricity consumption, with data centres alone consuming over 42 million megawatt-hours, underlining the infrastructure cost of the AI arms race.

Why it matters: AI export control risk is real and moving quickly; data centre power demand (42mn MWh/yr for Google alone) is reshaping energy market dynamics — directly relevant to utilities and power infrastructure investment themes.
