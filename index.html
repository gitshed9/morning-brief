OpenAI proposes 5% stake (~$42.6bn) to US government

OpenAI is reportedly in talks with the Trump administration — including Commerce Secretary Lutnick and Treasury Secretary Bessent — about the US government taking an equity stake in the company, valued at roughly $42.6bn. The proposal would mark an unusual direct government equity position in a leading AI lab.

Why it matters: Would set a precedent for government equity in critical AI infrastructure, with implications for competitive dynamics and regulatory oversight across the sector.

TSMC posts record AI-driven revenue

TSMC reported record revenue on the back of surging AI chip demand, reinforcing that the AI hardware buildout is converting into real, bookable orders rather than speculative capacity commitments.

Why it matters: A read-through for the broader semiconductor supply chain and a supportive data point for the overnight SMH +2.5% rebound noted in Section 1B.

Anthropic in talks with Samsung on custom Claude chip; IPO prep reported

Anthropic is reportedly discussing a custom AI chip with Samsung and is said to be preparing an S-1 filing for a potential IPO as early as October 2026.

Why it matters: Signals AI labs increasingly vertically integrating on silicon and moving toward public listings, both reshaping the competitive and capital-markets landscape for the sector.

Google caps Meta's Gemini access; NY pauses new hyperscale data centres

Google has capped Meta's access to its Gemini models citing capacity constraints, while New York became the first US state to pause approvals for new hyperscale data centres. Both point to compute and power as binding constraints on AI scaling. Ahead this week: Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro launch and the Shanghai WAIC conference (17 July), with Xi Jinping attending.

Why it matters: Capacity rationing and regulatory pushback on data centre buildout are emerging as real constraints on the AI infrastructure growth story, alongside continued model-release cadence from major labs.
