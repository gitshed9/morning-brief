12 — WHAT'S HAPPENING IN TECH

OpenAI Launches GPT-5.5-Cyber and “Patch the Planet” — Direct Counter to Anthropic Glasswing

OpenAI launched GPT-5.5-Cyber (85.6% on CyberGym vs 81.8% for standard GPT-5.5) and the “Patch the Planet” open-source security initiative with Trail of Bits. A five-day sprint across 30+ projects (Linux, Python, cURL, Sigstore) found hundreds of vulnerabilities, generated 8 kernel pointer info-leak proofs and 24 local privilege escalation exploits. The Codex Security plugin embeds vulnerability scanning directly into developer workflows. OpenAI positions Daybreak as its answer to Anthropic’s Project Glasswing — both labs are betting cybersecurity is the most politically defensible frontier AI use case.

Why it matters: AI-native security is becoming a distinct platform battle — whoever owns the developer’s IDE and security workflow owns a defensible enterprise moat.

SpaceX Signs $6.3B Reflection AI Compute Deal — Colossus 2 Now $80B+ in Committed Revenue

Reflection AI (Nvidia-backed, $25B valuation) signed a $150M/month deal through 2029 for GB300 chip access at SpaceX’s Colossus 2 in Memphis. SpaceX now has $80B+ in committed compute revenue — Anthropic pays $1.25B/month, Google $920M/month. SPCX stock fell 10% on concerns: all contracts carry a 90-day exit clause, compute-as-a-service margins are structurally thin, and the $60B Cursor acquisition has yet to demonstrate revenue synergy.

Why it matters: SpaceX is becoming the fourth major AI cloud alongside AWS, Azure, and Google — with every major AI lab now a paying customer, but margin quality is the open question.

China Announces $295B Five-Year AI Infrastructure Plan

The Chinese government unveiled a $295 billion, five-year AI infrastructure commitment — one of the largest government AI plans in history, spanning compute, data centers, and AI integration across state enterprises. Separately, 42 US state attorneys general opened a sweeping investigation into OpenAI covering advertising claims, sycophancy issues, data handling, and treatment of minors. OpenAI is targeting a September 2026 public offering.

Why it matters: Government-scale AI investment is accelerating globally while US labs face mounting regulatory scrutiny — a combination that favors infrastructure plays (data centers, power, chips) over pure-model businesses.

Anthropic Fable 5 Now Requires Paid Credits; Google Releases Gemini 2.5 Pro with Deep Think

June 23 marks the first day Fable 5 requires usage credits ($10/M input tokens, $50/M output tokens — double Opus 4.8 pricing). Anthropic made no extension for the six days the model was offline during the June 12–18 export-control suspension. Enterprise customers are now budgeting Fable 5 for the first time, and developer discussion focuses on when its quality justifies the premium vs routing to cheaper models. Google also released Gemini 2.5 Pro with a new “Deep Think” reasoning mode, its most capable model to date.

Why it matters: Frontier model pricing is entering a new phase — enterprises will route tasks by capability tier, and Fable 5’s pricing at 2× Opus 4.8 will test whether quality commands a sustained premium.
