12 — WHAT'S HAPPENING IN TECH

Anthropic Disables Claude Fable 5 & Mythos 5 Following US Export Control Directive

On June 9, Anthropic launched Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 — a new capability tier above Claude Opus 4.8. Three days later, the US government issued an export control directive citing national security concerns: a jailbreak technique had been identified that could unlock Mythos's cybersecurity capabilities. Because Anthropic could not filter foreign nationals from domestic users in real time, it disabled both models globally on June 12. The ban extends to foreign nationals inside the US, including Anthropic's own employees.

Why it matters: First direct US export ban on a commercial AI model; raises serious questions about AI geopolitics and the competitive position of US labs vs Chinese counterparts. Anthropic disputes the scope of the jailbreak cited by the government.

EU Selects EUROPA Consortium for Sovereign Frontier AI Model

On June 19, the European Commission named the EUROPA Consortium — led by Italian enterprise Domyn — as the winner of its Frontier AI Grand Challenge. The project will build a sovereign, open-source model exceeding 400 billion parameters, trained natively across all 24 official EU languages using a dedicated 6,000-chip NVIDIA Blackwell cluster and up to 2.5% of EuroHPC's supercomputing capacity.

Why it matters: The EU is actively decoupling from US AI infrastructure; a 400B+ parameter open-source European model could materially shift enterprise AI procurement decisions and disrupt OpenAI/Anthropic's European revenue.

Apple Signs $1B/Year AI Licensing Deal; Siri Redesigned Around Private Compute

Apple signed a $1 billion annual licensing agreement with an undisclosed AI partner and rebuilt Siri around a private compute architecture, prioritising on-device processing and hardware integration. The move positions Apple as an AI platform operator that keeps model inference away from third-party cloud infrastructure.

Why it matters: Apple's push into private AI compute creates demand for edge inference silicon and could affect cloud AI service providers' enterprise penetration.

SpaceX ($SPCX) Sheds $600B from Post-IPO Highs; Issues $20B+ in Investment-Grade Bonds

SpaceX has fallen for three consecutive sessions from its post-IPO peak, shedding over $600 billion in market capitalisation and slipping below $2 trillion. Despite the selloff, the company is issuing at least $20 billion in investment-grade bonds — its first major debt offering — to fund AI ambitions, and signed a compute supply agreement with Reflection AI startup.

Why it matters: The largest post-IPO drawdown on record signals AI infrastructure valuations are repricing; SpaceX's ability to issue $20B in investment-grade paper at 3 days of losses tests whether institutional appetite for AI capital structures remains intact.
