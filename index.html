12 — WHAT'S HAPPENING IN TECH

Apple sues OpenAI over alleged trade-secret theft tied to hiring spree

Apple filed suit against OpenAI on 11 July in the Northern District of California, alleging trade-secret theft connected to OpenAI's hiring of more than 400 former Apple silicon, on-device-AI and hardware staff.

Why it matters: escalates the AI talent war between two of the largest tech players and could constrain cross-pollination of engineering talent between Apple's hardware/on-device AI teams and frontier AI labs — a legal precedent here would reshape how aggressively labs can poach specialist staff.

Meta's leaked compute-doubling memo drives best week since early 2024

An internal memo revealed Meta plans to double total compute capacity by 2027, including a $10bn, 1GW data centre in Alberta, Canada. The stock rallied 6-7% Friday and posted its best week since early 2024 (~+15%); BofA reiterated its Buy rating.

Why it matters: signals continued aggressive AI capex escalation among hyperscalers even as investors question ROI on prior spend — a bullish read for AI infrastructure/power suppliers, a cost/margin question for Meta shareholders long-term.

SK Hynix's SKHY debuts as largest-ever foreign US listing

SK Hynix's US ADR (SKHY) raised $28-29bn in its debut — the largest foreign listing in US history — and soared on its first trading day. The company controls roughly 60% of the global HBM (high-bandwidth memory) market, a critical input for AI accelerators.

Why it matters: deepens US investor access to the HBM supply chain that underpins the Nvidia/AI hardware complex and could recalibrate memory-sector valuations globally, including AU-listed semiconductor-adjacent exposure.

Fed launches formal AI-economic-impact study body; Gemini 3.5 Pro leak; SambaNova raises $1bn

The Federal Reserve announced its first formal body dedicated to studying AI's economic impact, co-led by Marc Andreessen. Separately, Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro (2M-token context) reportedly leaked ahead of a 17 July general availability date, and SambaNova closed a $1bn Series F at an $11bn valuation.

Why it matters: institutional-level attention from the Fed underscores how central AI has become to macro policy thinking; the Gemini leak and SambaNova raise keep the AI-model and infrastructure funding cycle firmly in focus heading into CPI week.
