12 — WHAT'S HAPPENING IN TECH

Alphabet's AI Brain Drain: Shazeer Leaves for OpenAI, Jumper Leaves for Anthropic

On June 18, Noam Shazeer — VP of Engineering and co-lead of Google's Gemini models, who Google paid $2.7bn to reacquire via the Character.AI buyback in 2024 — announced his departure to OpenAI. Over the weekend, DeepMind VP John Jumper (AlphaFold2 lead researcher, Nobel Prize-adjacent breakthrough) announced he was leaving for Anthropic. The back-to-back exits erased roughly $250bn from Alphabet's market cap on June 22. Alphabet is simultaneously raising $80bn through equity to fund $180–190bn in AI capital expenditure in 2026, raising investor questions about returns on that outlay without its key architects.

Why it matters: The most valuable AI talent in the world is voting with its feet — away from Google and toward OpenAI and Anthropic. This is both a competitive moat signal and a read on which organisations are winning the AI arms race.

Anthropic Launches Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 — Then the US Restricts Export

Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 on June 9, 2026 — a significant capability tier above Claude Opus 4.8. Just three days later, on June 12, the US government issued an urgent export-control directive barring access to the models by foreign nationals, citing national security concerns. This marks the first time AI model access has been directly subject to export-control restrictions in the US, blurring the line between AI development and national security policy.

Why it matters: AI capability is now an explicit geopolitical instrument — the US is weaponising model access the same way it restricted semiconductor exports. Investors in global AI infrastructure (data centres, compute, GPU supply chains) should watch regulatory escalation closely.

OpenAI Partner Network: $150M and a Target of 300,000 Certified Consultants by End-2026

OpenAI launched the OpenAI Partner Network — a formal ecosystem for consultants and integrators backed by $150 million and structured around tiered certification. The target is 300,000 certified consultants by the end of 2026. The program is modelled partly on Salesforce's ISV/partner ecosystem, which took a decade to build. OpenAI is trying to compress that into 12 months by leveraging the existing demand for GPT-4/5-era enterprise deployments.

Why it matters: This is the most concrete signal yet that OpenAI is building an enterprise-grade distribution moat — shifting from a research lab with an API to a platform with a partner ecosystem. Enterprise software incumbents (MSFT, Salesforce, ServiceNow) are watching closely.

Great American AI Act: 269-Page Draft Proposes 3-Year Federal Preemption of State AI Laws

Representatives Obernolte and Trahan unveiled a 269-page discussion draft of the Great American Artificial Intelligence Act, which includes a three-year preemption of state AI laws related to frontier model development. The bill is designed to prevent a patchwork of 50 different state-level AI regulations from fragmenting the US AI development environment — giving federal regulators sole jurisdiction over foundation model governance during the draft period.

Why it matters: Federal preemption removes the immediate regulatory risk for US AI developers that a California or New York-style AI law would impose. Short-term positive for frontier model companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta AI); longer-term, federal oversight will likely be stricter once the preemption period ends.
