12 — WHAT'S HAPPENING IN TECH

Microsoft executes largest-ever Xbox restructuring

Microsoft cut roughly 3,200 gaming roles (~20% of the Xbox workforce) and divested four studios — Compulsion Games, Double Fine, Ninja Theory and Undead Labs — while Arkane explores strategic options. It marks the deepest structural pullback in the division's history.

Why it matters: Signals continued cost discipline in big-tech gaming/content spend even amid AI capex growth elsewhere — a datapoint for gaming-adjacent portfolio names.

Samsung's earnings miss triggers global chip rout

Samsung shares fell 9.1% despite quarterly profit rising 19-fold year-on-year, as results missed lofty AI-driven expectations. The reaction triggered a Kospi circuit breaker (-4.91%, intraday -8%) and dragged the SOX index down 4.65% overnight.

Why it matters: A stark reminder that AI-linked hardware names now trade on razor-thin expectation beats; expect continued volatility into upcoming semis earnings and a cautious ASX Tech open today.

Meta launches Muse image-generation model

Meta rolled out its new Muse image-generation model inside the Meta AI app on 7 July, continuing the platform's push to embed generative tools directly into consumer products.

Why it matters: Adds to the competitive intensity in consumer generative AI, relevant context for megacap tech positioning amid the broader AI-spend rotation debate.

AI governance and chip self-sufficiency both advance

The UN's Global Dialogue on AI Governance in Geneva (6-7 July) pushed for coordinated international oversight amid warnings of "catastrophic harm," while separately researchers detailed JADEPUFFER, a fully autonomous LLM-driven ransomware lifecycle. In parallel, India's Modi opened a new CG Semi OSAT facility in Gujarat and DeepSeek is reportedly developing its own AI chip as Chinese firms pivot from Nvidia toward domestic silicon.

Why it matters: Regulatory tightening and accelerating non-US chip self-sufficiency are structural themes for global semis exposure and cyber-risk monitoring.
