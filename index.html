12 — WHAT'S HAPPENING IN TECH

Meta cuts ~8,000 roles as AI agent push stalls

Meta laid off roughly 8,000 staff (~10% of its workforce) on 2 July while reassigning 7,000 employees onto AI teams. Zuckerberg publicly acknowledged the company's AI agent program had stalled for four months despite heavy spend.

Why it matters: Even hyperscalers are struggling to convert AI capex into shipped product — watch the read-across to AI infrastructure demand assumptions.

Chinese AI models grab a third or more of enterprise API usage

Chinese-developed AI models now account for 30-46% of enterprise API token usage on US developer platforms. Z.ai's GLM-5.2 saw roughly 80x customer growth in its first week available on Vercel.

Why it matters: Rising price/performance competition from Chinese labs could compress margins across the US AI software stack.

US AI policy remains unsettled as signing ceremony is scrapped

A White House voluntary AI standards framework was expected in the 8-11 July window but the planned signing ceremony was cancelled. The 1 August deadline for NSA/CISA classified frontier-model benchmarks still stands.

Why it matters: Regulatory direction for US AI policy is still unresolved — a source of ongoing headline risk for AI-exposed names.

Frontier model pricing moves higher as usage-based billing expands

Usage-credit billing for a new flagship model began 8 July at $10/M input and $50/M output tokens — roughly double the prior top-tier model's pricing. A rival's next-generation model remains in limited enterprise preview with no confirmed launch date.

Why it matters: Rising frontier-model pricing supports the monetisation thesis for the AI compute complex.
