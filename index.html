12 — WHAT'S HAPPENING IN TECH

Meta's custom AI chip "Iris" heads to manufacturing in September

Meta, working with Broadcom and TSMC, will begin manufacturing its custom "Iris" AI chip in September after completing testing in just six weeks. Meta plans to scale to 7GW of compute in 2026 and 14GW by 2027, signalling a push to reduce reliance on Nvidia.

Why it matters: Accelerating in-house silicon from a hyperscaler is a direct read-through to Nvidia's competitive moat and the broader AI capex supply chain.

OpenAI launches GPT-5.6 family, ChatGPT Work desktop app and GPT-Live voice

OpenAI released the GPT-5.6 model family alongside a ChatGPT Work desktop app and GPT-Live, a full-duplex voice mode supporting real-time translation, live search and agent delegation.

Why it matters: The enterprise/desktop push and agentic voice features intensify competition with Microsoft Copilot and Google Workspace AI tools.

Apple sues OpenAI over trade secrets amid Musk-Altman feud

Apple filed suit against OpenAI on 11 July alleging trade-secret misappropriation tied to OpenAI's hiring of more than 400 ex-Apple staff. The filing coincided with a public feud between Elon Musk and Sam Altman playing out on X.

Why it matters: Escalating legal and personal conflict among major AI players raises talent-war and litigation risk across the sector.

US VC funding hits $412.7bn in H1 2026, AI is 86% of it

US venture funding reached $412.7bn in the first half of 2026, already up 30% on all of 2025, with AI accounting for 86% of the total. Separately, AWS is standing up a $1bn internal AI deployment organisation, Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro is expected 17 July, and Shanghai's WAIC opened with Xi Jinping attending.

Why it matters: The scale of AI capital concentration keeps raising bubble-risk questions even as hyperscaler capex and Chinese state-level AI investment continue to accelerate.
