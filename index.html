12 — WHAT'S HAPPENING IN TECH

Samsung + SK Group Announce US$880bn Korean Chipmaking Investment

Samsung and SK Group unveiled plans to invest US$880bn in building chipmaking plants in southwest Korea, with the South Korean government committing to double memory production capacity within 5 years and support projects with power and water infrastructure. The news sent ASML +6.8%, KLA +8.4%, and Applied Materials +10.1% overnight — the largest single-session move for SemiCap names in months.

Why it matters: This is a generational commitment to AI-driven semiconductor infrastructure — positioning Korea as the dominant memory production hub for the next decade, and directly validating the bull case for US SemiCap equipment names.

Qualcomm in Early Talks to Acquire Tenstorrent for US$8-10bn

Qualcomm is in early-stage discussions to acquire Tenstorrent, a RISC-V-based AI chip designer, for between US$8bn and US$10bn. The deal would signal a major Qualcomm commitment to competitive AI infrastructure, moving beyond mobile processors into the AI accelerator market dominated by NVIDIA. Tenstorrent's open RISC-V architecture is seen as a more licensable alternative to proprietary chip designs.

Why it matters: A Qualcomm-Tenstorrent deal would reshape the AI chip competitive landscape, creating a better-resourced challenger to NVIDIA's CUDA ecosystem and validating the RISC-V movement as a serious AI infrastructure alternative.

Meta Begins 8,000-Person Layoff Round as AI Restructuring Accelerates

Meta commenced layoffs of approximately 8,000 employees — around 10% of its total workforce — as part of a sweeping AI-focused restructuring. An additional 7,000 employees have been reassigned to AI-centric teams. The cuts add to over 100,000 tech industry job losses in 2026, many attributed directly to AI automation replacing traditional roles. Meta has also confirmed an expanded partnership with NVIDIA on next-generation AI infrastructure.

Why it matters: Meta is aggressively reorienting to AI at scale — the combination of workforce reduction and AI investment signals that the productivity gains from AI are arriving fast enough to justify dramatic org restructuring at the world's largest social networks.

US Government to Use ChatGPT for Federal Health Spending Audit

The US Department of Health and Human Services announced it will deploy ChatGPT and other AI tools to continuously analyse annual audit reports from all 50 states on fraud, waste, and abuse in federal health spending. The program represents one of the largest government deployments of commercial AI tools for regulatory oversight, and is being positioned as a way to cut wasteful spending across Medicare and Medicaid programs.

Why it matters: Government AI adoption at this scale validates the enterprise AI rollout thesis — AI moving from demos to genuine institutional deployment, with OpenAI securing a flagship government customer that legitimises the technology for procurement officers globally.
